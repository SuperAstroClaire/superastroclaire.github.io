Asymmetries in Stellar Streams Induced by a Galactic Merger

This project explores how a 1:10 galactic merger can disturb and reshape the stellar streams produced by globular clusters orbiting a Milky-Way-like galaxy. The study is presented in our paper Asymmetries in stellar streams induced by a galactic merger (Guillaume et al., 2025, submitted). Using a collisionless N-body simulation, we followed 36 globular clusters over 10 Gyr to quantify how a merging satellite perturbs their tidal tails.

The animation below shows the full simulation: initially, the globular clusters orbit around the host galaxy. Stellar streams gradually form as clusters lose stars. As time advances, a massive satellite approaches, crosses the disk twice, and finally merges, triggering gaps, folds, and asymmetries in the stellar streams. At the end, the camera rotates around the system, highlighting each stream in color.

Individual Streams and Their Perturbations

The simulation reveals a rich variety of merger-induced stream morphologies, including gaps, folds, bifurcations, fanning structures, asymmetries, and regions of over- and under-density.
