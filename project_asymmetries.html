Asymmetries in Stellar Streams Induced by a Galactic Merger

This project explores how a 1:10 galactic merger can disturb and reshape the stellar streams produced by globular clusters orbiting a Milky-Way-like galaxy. The study is presented in our paper Asymmetries in stellar streams induced by a galactic merger (Guillaume et al., 2025, submitted). Using a collisionless N-body simulation, we followed 36 globular clusters over 10 Gyr to quantify how a merging satellite perturbs their tidal tails.

The animation below shows the full simulation: initially, the globular clusters orbit in smooth symmetry around the host galaxy. As time advances, a massive satellite approaches, crosses the disk twice, and finally merges, triggering gaps, folds, and asymmetries in the stellar streams. At the end, the camera rotates around the system, highlighting each stream in color.

Individual Streams and Their Perturbations

Below, two representative streams are shown. Each row corresponds to one stream: the left panel shows a still image, while the right panel displays a short animation of its evolution during and after the merger. Notice the emergence of gaps and asymmetries in the leading and trailing arms.

These visualizations illustrate how galactic mergers can leave long-lasting signatures in the morphology of stellar streams—an important consideration for using streams to map the Milky Way’s dark matter distribution.
